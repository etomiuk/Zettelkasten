The role of experience in composition

There is no doubt that our experiences can shape a composer's compositions. These experiences can be of many forms: they can relate to the culture they belong to, the people they encounter, their teachers, world events that affect them, or even just mundane experiences from daily life. There is simply no way that a work can be understood without taking into account someone's experience, whether it be the individual's personal experiences, or the experience of a collective group.
