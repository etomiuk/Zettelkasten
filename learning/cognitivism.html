How do we learn to compose?

Cognitivism: changing mental processes when composing

The cognitivist view about learning claims that learning is a change in mental processes (Lecture 3). Some of these mental processes include attention, memory, knowledge processing and organization. It's all about creating the mental connections with knowledge that is already stored in long term memory. Learning is also applying information learnt to different situations. ***form the connection to deep processing

Criticism

One flaw of the cognitive perspective is that it ignores the influence of the environment on our learning, something that is addressed by social cognitivists. Other composers that we enjoy will influence our composition style. Not getting any feedback on your compositions might not allow you to learn as much because you don't get perspectives and advice that can allow you to focus your attention on certain aspects of composition. ***link to attention and social and mentorship
