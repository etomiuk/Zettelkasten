How do we learn to compose?

Constructivism: composers creating new knowledge through music

According to constructivists, learning happens by making your own meaning from your experiences (Ertmer & Newby, 2013). Vygostsky's social constructivist theory claims that this knowledge is created as a group - you need the help from others to create knowledge (Lecture 6). In a way, by creating new ways of combining sounds and melodies, they create new knowledge, new ways of expressing a certain idea that others can use and modify to create their own unique interpretation. In this sense, one can learn to compose by creating music that relates to their personal experiences ***link to experience, social cognitivism, motivation (intrinsic)

Criticisms

How can we show that we construct knowledge? What about behavioural displays of behaviour?
