Connectivism: Composing with technology

Connectivism is a general framework in which learning depends on a network of nodes and connections that are affected by technology (Lecture 11). These nodes can be things like ideas, other people, knowledge, etc. Essentially, technology affects the way we create ideas and share them with other people and these ideas get distributed through a large network.

The fact that these nodes can be other people emphasizes the fact that learning is very social (link to social cognitivism).

Criticism

No research supports this as a learning theory since it is just a rough idea that combines many theories. Also, it neglects the mechanisms through which we really learn, something that is covered in other theories like behaviourism and cognitivism (add links).
