Learning Theories

Humanism: composition as a way to fulfill your potential

The humanist view of learning claims that the point of learning is to become better versions of ourselves (Lecture 7). In this view, learning should always be centered about fulfilling our potential and goals (link to goals here). This is described in Maslow's hierarchy of needs, where the top of this pyramid is self-actualization. Maslow's pyramid implies that we should strive to attain self-actualization, which is being able to master something that matters to us.
